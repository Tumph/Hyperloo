Course Description

CO 255:

Linear optimization: feasibility theorems, duality, the simplex algorithm. Discrete optimization: integer linear programming, cutting planes, network flows. Continuous optimization: local and global optima, feasible directions, convexity, necessary optimality conditions.

View requirements for CO 255

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Formulate optimization problems as linear, nonlinear, or integer programming.
Know a variety of optimization algorithms, including the Simplex method, cutting plane methods, and descent methods.
Know and apply the definition and properties of convex sets and functions.
Write the dual of a linear programming problem and understand the properties relating the primal and dual.
Apply optimization methods to common continuous and discrete problems including game theory, flow, scheduling, and data science.

Tentative Course Schedule

  1. Overview of optimization.
  2. Linear programming, Fourier-Motzkin elimination, and Farkas's lemma.
  3. The simplex method.
  4. Duality in linear programming.
  5. Convex sets and functions.
  6. Convex optimization and the KKT conditions.
  7. Discrete optimization and total unimodularity.
  8. Applications of total unimodularity to matching and flow problems
  9. Descent methods
  10. Application of nonlinear optimization to data science
